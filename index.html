Mood Memo

As one of my initial independent projects on Xcode using SwiftUI, I found myself eager to craft something practical and well-suited for daily use. Given the popularity of journaling and lifestyle tracking, I decided to take my first steps into this realm.

The project is currently tailored for iOS 16.4, but I have plans to enhance its capabilities for iOS 17. This will involve integrating widgets to seamlessly interact with the core features of the application.

Users can assign colors or 'Moods' to calendar days, along with writing memos stored by date. Editing is intuitive via quick actions or opening an edit sheet.

This app is a work in progress, with frameworks for mood editing and default view switching still being built. Sticking with the idea that dedicated memo and mood editing views will enhance interface navigation, I plan to implement those as well.

48 Hour Game Jams

These are the games I worked on for University of Utah GameCraft club's game jams. As the lead artist, I gained proficiency in Unity and 2D sprite integration.

"Getting it Together": My first game jam experience, a challenging yet enjoyable platformer where you help a robot escape a factory.

"Rakka": A narrative-driven visual novel with elements of horror. I led the visual and narrative direction, resulting in a successfully completed project despite time constraints.

"Candy Reaper": An experimental PvP game inspired by Smash Bros. We opted for a nuanced approach through team discussions. Victory goes to the player with the most collected candy, though we didn't quite finish all the mechanics.

"CCP": A polished side-scrolling bullet hell shooter. Leading visual development and collaborating with engineers, we created an engaging game. Try to beat level 4 - even the development team couldn't reach level 5!

"Cannibal Starfish": Influenced by the theme "Be There in Five," this game challenged me to integrate mesh mechanics and softbody physics for a cohesive visual experience.

Website Portfolio

I crafted this website using Visual Studio Code, employing HTML, CSS, and Javascript. Having prior experience with platforms like WordPress and Adobe Portfolio, I discovered coding a website to be more liberating and expressive.

While not overly intricate, I aimed for a clean and minimalist design, drawing inspiration from Keita Yamada's work with some personal tweaks.

A highlight was implementing Javascript, particularly creating a dynamic floating effect responsive to mouse movements.

Delving into particle art and Javascript-based art was enthralling, as it's entirely founded on mathematical principles.

A canvas lets my imagination freely construct visual narratives and compelling stories. Through mental visualization, and creative thinking, art has unexpectedly honed my approach to writing code.
